What it does

Jobs board

Track roles from any source in one place. Status, timeline, and next steps — all visible at a glance.

AI-assisted

Score opportunities for fit, surface relevant contacts, and draft outreach — without outsourcing the judgment.

Contacts

Log every person and company involved. Track relationships, warm connections, and touchpoints over time.

History

Every stage, decision, and follow-up is timestamped. Never lose context on a role you were pursuing months ago.

Scoring

Rate each role across criteria that matter to you — scope, team, comp, growth — with adjustable weights.

Private & portable

Lives in Notion. No SaaS lock-in, no recruiter-facing dashboard. Your data, fully in your control.

Why I built it

During an extended job search I couldn't find a tool that treated the process seriously. Most trackers were basic spreadsheets, and the SaaS options were optimized for recruiters, not candidates. They didn't help with the hard parts: evaluating whether a role was actually a fit, remembering who was connected to what, or knowing what deserved attention that week.

I wanted something that would let me think clearly about fit, track history rigorously, and use AI selectively — to surface signal, not replace judgment. Work Radar is what I built to run my own search. It's Notion-native so it stays flexible, and because I built it for myself, it's opinionated in exactly the ways I needed.

How it works

1 Capture

Add a role, paste a job description, tag the source. Takes under a minute.

2 Evaluate

AI scores the role against your criteria. You review and adjust weights to match what actually matters.

3 Track contacts

Link people and companies. Note how you know them, warm connections, and who's already in your network.

4 Move through stages

Outreach → screen → interview → offer, with full history at every transition.

5 Review the board

A dashboard view surfaces what's gone stale, what's active, and where to focus your energy.

6 Weekly summary

Generate a snapshot of pipeline activity, high-priority roles, and next steps — useful for staying honest with yourself.

Inside Work Radar

Notes from the build

  • Notion's formula fields are more capable than they look but hit limits on complex multi-step scoring — worth knowing before you lean on them heavily.
  • The hardest UX problem was surfacing "what needs attention today" without overwhelming the board overview. Filters saved it.
  • Scoring is opinionated. The default weights are mine; they're easy to change but most people won't. That's probably fine.
  • AI-drafted outreach gets about 60% of the way there. The remaining 40% is judgment about tone, timing, and what the person actually cares about.
  • History is underrated. Being able to look back at why I passed on a role — or why I was excited — turned out to be genuinely useful for calibrating over time.
