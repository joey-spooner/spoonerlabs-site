Insights

Who Always Votes Blue?

14 counties voted Democratic in every gubernatorial primary on record — including years when Republicans ran contested races. These include the state capital (Leon), Florida's largest university town (Alachua/Gainesville), majority-Black counties in the historic Black Belt (Gadsden, Jefferson, Madison, Hamilton), and South Florida population centers (Broward, Palm Beach).

Alachua · Broward · Calhoun · DeSoto · Franklin · Gadsden · Hamilton · Jackson · Jefferson · Leon · Liberty · Madison · Palm Beach · Taylor

Who Always Votes Red?

No county voted Republican in every election in this dataset — because the 2002 and 2022 Republican primaries were uncontested (Jeb Bush and Ron DeSantis ran without opposition), leaving only Democratic primary votes to color those maps. That's a data artifact, not a political shift.

In the five years when both parties held contested primaries (1994, 2006, 2010, 2014, 2018), 20 counties voted Republican every time: suburban Central Florida, the Gulf Coast, and the Space Coast.

Brevard · Charlotte · Clay · Collier · Flagler · Hernando · Highlands · Indian River · Lake · Lee · Manatee · Marion · Martin · Okaloosa · Pasco · Pinellas · Sarasota · Seminole · St. Johns · Volusia

Methodology & Sources

Legislature data

Combined Florida House and Senate partisan composition, 1992–2024. Sourced from Ballotpedia Florida House and Senate partisan history tables. Pre-2006 data compiled from Michael Dubin's historical records.

Florida House: 120 seats. Florida Senate: 40 seats. Total: 160 seats per cycle.

Gubernatorial primary data

County-level results from the Florida Division of Elections. 2014, 2018, and 2022 sourced from precinct-level ZIP files (dos.fl.gov). 1994, 2002, 2006, and 2010 sourced via bulk TSV extract from the Florida DoE results archive (results.elections.myflorida.com).

County coloring

Each county is colored by which major party (Democrat or Republican) drew the higher total votes across its gubernatorial primary contest. When only one party held a contested primary (e.g., 2002 and 2022 Republican primaries were uncontested), the county is colored by the party with available votes.

Excluded years

1998: Both primaries were uncontested. The US Election Atlas data for 1998 was found to display general election results (Bush vs. MacKay) mislabeled as primary data. Year excluded from the county map; legislature data is included in the chart.

1992: No gubernatorial primary was held in this cycle. Legislature data shown.
